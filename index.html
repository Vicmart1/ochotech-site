Background
In most clinical settings, physicians often rely on the patient's self-report when assessing medication adherence. However, patients forget and tend to overreport, giving physicians a skewed understanding of their actual adherence levels.



So, we did some research...
After conducting thorough literary review and user studies, we focused on medication adherence with Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) patients. Of those patients:
Our results show that more likely than not, Coronary Artery Disease patients are not telling the truth about their medication adherence.



Thus, we believe that physicians need a way to verify that CAD patients are taking their medications as prescribed.
